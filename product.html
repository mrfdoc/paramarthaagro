Coconut Charcoal Briquettes

Indonesia is one of the largest coconut producers in the world with a very distinctive quality. The use of coconut, of course, besides being consumed as a food ingredient, it can also be made into charcoal briquettes.

The briquette charcoal produced from this coconut is extraordinary, has the perfect quality to be used as a substitute for fuel which of course has the advantage of a longer shelf life compared to ordinary charcoal. In addition, the use of coconut shell charcoal makes the briquettes have a fairly high burning heat and also less smoke, odorless and of course we have been certified.

Description Specification Certificates

Description

We make coconut shell charcoal briquettes for Shisha / hookah and for barbeque use. Our briquettes are made using only the highest quality coconut shell charcoal (which we produce ourselves), with a high carbon content and low ash content. The higher the fixed carbon, the higher the heat produced, the longer the briquette burns and the less ash is generated during combustion. Our coconut shell briquettes are hard and durable thanks to the high pressure used in production and the close monitoring of the drying process. Our briquettes are uniform and neat and can be made in various shapes and sizes. Strict quality control at all stages of production ensures consistently high quality. We can also pack briquettes according to your requirements.

Go to Spec.

Function

Charcoal briquettes are charcoal made from other types of charcoal which are mashed first and then printed as needed with a mixture of starch. The purpose of making charcoal briquettes is to increase the burn period and to save costs. Coconut shell charcoal can be used as charcoal briquettes, but only the crumbled shell. Meanwhile, an intact shell does not need to be turned into charcoal briquettes. Coconut shell charcoal briquettes are an alternative fuel that is often used for cooking, especially for baking foodstuffs and for shisha pipe smoking. Coconut shell charcoal briquettes produce more heat than coal briquettes. In addition, briquettes from coconut shell charcoal are also safer, environmentally friendly, because they do not cause smoke.

Description

The quality of Indonesian coffee beans produced does not disappoint consumers, especially for the European region. Coffee from the land of Java, which we know as Java Preanger, is so famous in Europe. Because of its popularity, Europeans often call it 'A Cup of Java' and no longer call it 'A Cup of Coffee' when drinking coffee. Starting until the 19th century, Java Preanger was the best coffee in the world. Java Preanger is often planted at an altitude of about 1000 meters above sea level according to the characteristics of Arabica coffee. Cultivation system, generally planted by individual farmers in Perhutani's garden areas where there are protective trees.

Go to Spec.

Characteristics

The character of Java Preanger coffee lies in its body and moderate acidity with a rich aroma and taste. This coffee is also known as Malabar coffee because it is taken from one of the names of the mountains in the coffee plantation area. Another reason is because it comes from seeds brought by the Dutch from Malabar, India.
Harvest Period: May - August
Cupping Notes: Chocolate, Caramel, Nutty, Peach, Berry, Sweet, Floral, Lemony, Balance after taste.
Productivity: 300 - 5000 Tons of green beans (Green Bean) per year.

Geographical Area

It is planted in highland areas with a minimum altitude of 900 meters above sea level (asl), Mount Cikuray, Mount Papandayan, Mount Malabar, Mount Caringin, Mount Tilu, Mount Patuha, Mount Halu, Mount Beser, Mount Burangrang, Mount Tangkuban Perahu and Mount Manglayang
Harvest Period: May - August.
Productivity: 300 - 5000 Tons of green beans (Green Bean) per year.
